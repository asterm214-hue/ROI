# ROI - Return on Investment Game Story

## Game Overview
**ROI** is an interactive financial education game that teaches players about money management, saving, investing, and financial decision-making through engaging story-driven quests. The game follows a young protagonist's financial journey from student pocket money to professional investment decisions.

---

## Level 1: Student Pocket Money Quest
**Objective:** Learn about Recurring Deposits (RD), Needs vs Wants, and long-term planning with ₹5,000 monthly pocket money.

### Story Flow

#### Opening Scene
- **Character:** Adam/Eve receives ₹5,000 monthly pocket money
- **Excitement:** "Yay! I finally got ₹5000 as my pocket money this month. I have so many things I want to buy!"
- **Decision Point:** Talk to Father about managing the money

#### Learning RD (Recurring Deposit)
- **Father's Advice:** "You shouldn't spend it all. You should save a part of it in an RD (Recurring Deposit)."
- **RD Explanation:** A way of saving regularly with monthly deposits that earn interest and build disciplined saving habits
- **Key Learning:** Regular saving builds financial security for future needs

#### Month 1: Savings Decision
- **Budget:** ₹5,000 available
- **Choices:**
  - **Save ₹2,000 (Recommended)** - Builds strong foundation, reduces risk
  - **Save ₹500** - Minimal savings, balanced approach
  - **Save ₹0** - Spend everything, maximizes happiness but increases risk

#### Social Temptation
- **Scene:** Friends invite to a ₹1,000 party
- **Choices:**
  - **Attend Party** - Social enjoyment but spending on "wants"
  - **Decline** - Financial discipline, focus on long-term goals
- **Key Learning:** Distinguish between needs and wants

#### Future Planning
- **Friends' Announcement:** College trip planned in 6 months costing ₹8,000
- **Choices:**
  - **Start saving extra ₹1,000/month** - Proactive planning
  - **Delay decision** - Risk of insufficient funds later
- **Key Learning:** Plan ahead for big expenses

#### Unexpected Need
- **Teacher Assignment:** Major project requiring ₹1,500 materials (mandatory)
- **Forced Decision:** Must spend on "needs" regardless of budget
- **Key Learning:** Some expenses are unavoidable necessities

#### 6-Month Time Jump
- **Evaluation:** Assess savings against ₹8,000 trip cost
- **Outcome:** Success depends on saving choices throughout the semester
- **Completion:** Level 1 complete - learned RD, needs vs wants, and planning

## Level 2: Salary Management Quest
**Objective:** Apply the 50-30-20 rule, avoid EMI traps, and build emergency funds with ₹50,000 monthly salary.

### Story Flow

#### Career Milestone
- **Character:** First salary of ₹50,000 received
- **Celebration:** "I just received my first salary — ₹50,000! I feel like I'm on top of the world."
- **Father's Guidance:** Introduces the 50-30-20 rule

#### The 50-30-20 Rule
- **Breakdown:**
  - **50% Needs:** Rent, bills, groceries (₹25,000)
  - **30% Wants:** Entertainment, hobbies, dining (₹15,000)
  - **20% Savings:** Future security and emergencies (₹10,000)
- **Key Learning:** Structured budgeting for balanced financial life

#### Budget Allocation Decision
- **Salary:** ₹50,000 monthly
- **Choices:**
  - **Follow 50-30-20 rule** - Balanced approach, saves ₹10,000
  - **Spend 80% on wants** - Maximum enjoyment, no savings, high risk

#### The EMI Trap
- **Temptation:** Friend shows ₹60,000 phone available for ₹5,000/month EMI
- **Choices:**
  - **Take EMI** - Instant gratification, but creates debt burden
  - **Save up instead** - Avoid debt trap, maintain financial freedom
- **Father's Warning:** Multiple EMIs can drain future monthly budgets
- **Key Learning:** EMIs seem affordable but create long-term financial strain

#### Financial Literacy Quiz
- **Challenge:** Classify items as NEEDS or WANTS
- **Examples:**
  - **Needs:** Rent, groceries, electricity bills
  - **Wants:** Designer shoes, gaming console, Netflix subscription
- **Key Learning:** Proper categorization of expenses

#### Emergency Fund Importance
- **Father's Advice:** "Always build an Emergency Fund. Life is unpredictable, and having 3-6 months of expenses saved can save you from high-interest loans later."
- **Key Learning:** Emergency funds prevent financial crises

#### 6-Month Crisis Test
- **Emergency:** Car breaks down, repair costs ₹20,000
- **Evaluation:** Can savings cover the emergency?
- **Outcome:** Depends on budgeting choices and emergency fund building
- **Completion:** Level 2 complete - mastered 50-30-20 rule, avoided EMI traps, understood emergency funds

## Level 3: The Investment Quest
**Objective:** Learn Debit vs Credit cards, understand interest, and explore investment options with ₹500,000 savings.

### Story Flow

#### Card Confusion
- **Scene:** Friend suggests splitting dinner bill
- **Temptation:** Use credit card for convenience
- **Question:** "Debit or credit... what's the real difference?"

#### Debit vs Credit Explanation
- **Debit Card:** Uses own money instantly from account
- **Credit Card:** Borrow money now, pay bank later (short-term loan)
- **Choices:**
  - **Use Debit** - Spend own money, maintain control
  - **Use Credit** - Convenience but creates debt obligation
- **Key Learning:** Credit cards are loans that must be repaid

#### Investment Awakening
- **Realization:** "Most of my money just sits in my savings account... Does it even do anything?"
- **Elder Brother:** Savings accounts grow slowly but safely
- **Question:** "Is there a way to make it grow faster?"

#### Investment Introduction
- **Friend's Success:** "I started investing in mutual funds and stocks and made some good profit!"
- **Father's Guidance:** Three investment options:
  - **FD (Fixed Deposit):** Safe, steady returns
  - **Mutual Funds:** Professional management, balanced risk
  - **Stocks:** High risk, high potential returns

#### Investment Decision
- **Capital:** ₹500,000 available
- **Choices:**
  - **Fixed Deposit** - Safe, reliable growth
  - **Mutual Funds** - Balanced professional approach
  - **Stocks** - High-risk, high-reward strategy

#### Market Results
- **Time Jump:** 3 months later
- **Evaluation:** Investment performance based on chosen strategy
- **Outcomes:** Vary by risk level and market conditions

#### Final Realization
- **Character Insight:** "I get it now. Saving keeps money safe, but investing helps it grow. And credit needs to be controlled wisely!"
- **Completion:** Level 3 complete - understood cards, interest, and investment basics

## Educational Themes

### Core Financial Concepts Covered:
1. **Saving vs Spending** - Building disciplined saving habits
2. **Needs vs Wants** - Prioritizing essential expenses
3. **Budgeting** - 50-30-20 rule for salary management
4. **Debt Management** - Avoiding EMI traps and credit card misuse
5. **Emergency Planning** - Building safety nets for unexpected expenses
6. **Investment Basics** - Understanding risk, return, and diversification
7. **Long-term Planning** - Preparing for future financial goals

### Character Development:
- **Level 1:** Innocent student learning basic money management
- **Level 2:** Young professional navigating salary and expenses
- **Level 3:** Financially aware individual exploring investment opportunities

### Game Mechanics:
- **Choice Impact:** Decisions affect Money, Happiness, and Risk stats
- **Mentor Guidance:** AI feedback provides learning reinforcement
- **Progressive Difficulty:** Each level builds on previous knowledge
- **Real Consequences:** Poor choices lead to financial challenges

---

## Story Progression Summary

**Level 1 → Level 2 → Level 3** represents a complete financial journey:
- From **basic saving** (pocket money) to **budgeting** (salary) to **investing** (wealth building)
- From **short-term thinking** to **long-term planning**
- From **spending habits** to **wealth creation**

Each level teaches critical financial literacy concepts through interactive storytelling, helping players develop real-world money management skills.
